Synopsis

The story of Dune follows the young Paul Atreides, a nobleman of House Atreides. He is forced to travel to the planet Arrakis, a desert wasteland which is the key to the future of his family and people.

Review

Rating: ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

Overall thoughts:

World

Dune's story is solid, and the world Herbert created is one that I think will stick with me for a very long time. The lore of this world and thorough and fascinating, and the journey taken by Paul Atreides throughout the novel is one that I will remember.


A complaint I and many others seem to have about this book is that from its first few chapters, you are thrown into the deep end; there are a lot of concepts and terminology that you are exposed to in the beginning, making it difficult to immerse oneself in this world due to the amount of information you are given all at once. Once you turn the final page, however, you are likely to find that it was worth it to push through that initial learning curve.


Character

Each major character of the novel is well-developed, and they each play important parts in the larger story. Aside from the main character, Paul, there is his father, Duke Leto, a man who has been tasked with leading his house and family on a mission to a dangerous planet by order of the emporer. He does his best with the hand he's been dealt, and when all seems lost, he manages to sacrifice his life for the good of his people.


Lady Jessica is Paul's mother and a member of the myserious group known as the Bene Gesserit. She must constantly balance her duty to the Bene Gesserit and her family. Throughout the novel, she shows strength and wisdom on her travels with Paul through the desert.
