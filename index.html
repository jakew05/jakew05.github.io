Dune - Frank Herbert

1st book in the Dune Chronicles.

Full Series: Dune, Dune Messiah, Children of Dune, God Emporer of Dune,
Heretics of Dune, Chapterhouse: Dune, and more.

Synopsis

The story of Dune follows the young Paul Atreides, a nobleman of House Atreides. He is forced to travel to the planet
Arrakis, a desert wasteland which is the key to the future of his family and people.

Review

Rating: ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

Overall thoughts:

Dune's story is solid, and the world Herbert created is one that I think will stick with me for a very long
time. The lore of this world is thorough and fascinating, and the journey taken by Paul Atreides throughout the novel is one
that I will remember.

A complaint I and many others seem to have about this book is that from its first few chapters, you are thrown into
the deep end; there are a lot of concepts and terminology that you are exposed to in the beginning, making it
difficult to immerse oneself in this world due to the amount of information you are given all at once. Once you
turn the final page, however, you are likely to find that it was worth it to push through that initial learning curve.
